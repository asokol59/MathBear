Euclid

B: 339 BCE, D:275 BCE (Gullberg,J., 1997)

A short list of contemporaries

  • Eratosthenes (c. 276-194 BCE)

List of Major Works

  • Elements
  • Data
  • On Divisions of Figures
  • Catoptrics
  • Phaenomena

Euclid, often referred to as the "Father of Geometry," was a Greek mathematician who lived around 300 BCE. He is best known for his work "Elements," a comprehensive compilation of the knowledge of geometry of his time. Euclid's Elements consists of 13 books covering various topics, including plane geometry, number theory, and solid geometry. His systematic approach and logical deductions laid the foundation for modern geometry and influenced mathematical thought for centuries. Euclid's work has been studied and revered for its clarity, rigor, and enduring impact on mathematics.
